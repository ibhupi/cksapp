Public Transport

Most subway lines in Tokyo start at around 5 am and last trains depart at around midnight. In Tokyo the rush hour is roughly 7.30am-9.30am and 5.30pm-7.30pm. There are not many night buses, so taxis are the only option in the early hours. Subway services are a little less frequent on weekends and public holidays. There are things you can do if you miss the last subway and have to wait until the first train the next morning.

PASMO/SUICA can be used to travel on normal bus lines in the Tokyo metropolitan area as well as some inter-city express buses and airport shuttle buses.










Taxi Service

Taxi fares have basic charges.
Taxis can be taken at the taxi stand.
Smoking is prohibited inside taxis.
There are taxis that accept credit cards.
Taxi’s door on the left side will open and close automatically.










Bike Rental

Visitors to the city can use the small wheeled electric bicycles by purchasing a one-day pass and bicycles can be returned on any of the numerous ports in each ward.

Police "KOBAN"

Koban Japanese Police Box are the ubiquitous urban police stations in Japan usually located next to train stations, shopping centres, busy intersections, but also throughout residential districts.
